# STATUTORY NOTES — Indian GST / CGST Law Driving the Print Templates

Every print template in this design is anchored in a specific section of Indian tax law. This document maps each document to its statutory basis so the implementing developer understands which fields are non-negotiable.

This is **not legal advice** — it's a design reference summarising the research that drove the visual decisions. The implementing team should verify against current law before shipping to production. The text below was current as of mid-2026; check for amendments before go-live.

---

## Acts and rules referenced

- **Central Goods and Services Tax Act 2017** (CGST Act)
- **CGST Rules 2017** (especially Rules 46, 48, 53, 55, 56)
- **Indian Contract Act 1872** (for sale orders)
- **Legal Metrology (Packaged Commodities) Rules 2011** (for MRP on shelf labels)
- **National Commission for Allied & Healthcare Professions Act 2021** (for optometrist registration)
- **Drugs and Cosmetics Rules 1945** (for contact lens dispensing)

## Per-document statutory basis

### 1. Tax Invoice (Rule 46 CGST)

Issued under **Sec. 31 CGST Act 2017 r/w Rule 46**. The rule mandates 16–21 fields:

1. Name, address, GSTIN of the supplier
2. Consecutive serial number (≤ 16 characters)
3. Date of issue
4. Name, address, GSTIN/UIN of recipient (if registered)
5. Name, address, address of delivery (if recipient unregistered + supply value ≥ ₹50,000)
6. HSN code (for goods) or SAC (for services)
7. Description of goods/services
8. Quantity (for goods) and unit
9. Total value of supply
10. Taxable value (after discount)
11. Rate of tax (CGST + SGST or IGST or UTGST or cess)
12. Amount of tax
13. Place of supply with state name and state code
14. Address of delivery, if different from place of supply
15. Whether the tax is payable on reverse-charge basis
16. Signature or digital signature of the supplier or authorized signatory

Additional under Rule 46 amendments:
- Quick Response (QR) code containing the IRN, if e-invoice applicable
- Endorsement on the invoice when issued for SEZ
- Amount in words

**Copy markers** per Rule 48(1): "Original for Recipient" / "Duplicate for Transporter" / "Triplicate for Supplier".

**E-invoice (Rule 48(4))**: Mandatory for taxpayers with aggregate turnover > ₹5cr. The IRN + QR is generated by IRP and must be printed on the invoice. Better Vision (the mock entity) is below this threshold, so the IRN field is marked `e-invoice exempt` — but the implementing developer should add IRN/QR support when crossing the threshold.

**B2C Dynamic QR (Notification 14/2020)**: Required if aggregate turnover > ₹500cr; not applicable to Better Vision but worth noting.

### 2. Delivery Challan (Rule 55 CGST)

Issued under **Rule 55(2) CGST Rules 2017**. Required when goods move without a tax invoice:
- Inter-branch stock transfer (same PAN)
- Job-work dispatch (lens to lab)
- Goods on approval / sale-or-return
- Liquid gas / fuel where qty not known at dispatch

**Triplicate mandate** per Rule 55(2):
- "Original for the Consignee"
- "Duplicate for the Transporter"
- "Triplicate for the Consignor"

Mandatory fields:
- Date and number
- Name, address, GSTIN of consignor
- Name, address, GSTIN (if registered) of consignee
- HSN code and description
- Quantity (provisional, where exact qty not known)
- Taxable value
- Tax rate and amount (CGST + SGST + IGST + cess)
- Place of supply
- Signature

**E-Way Bill**: Required for goods movement > ₹50,000 (consignment value). Generated on EWB portal; valid for 1 day per 200km. EWB number, generation timestamp, validity, distance shown on the challan.

### 3. Credit Note (Sec. 34(1) CGST)

A registered person may issue a credit note when:
- Taxable value or tax charged on the original invoice was excessive
- Goods are returned
- Goods supplied are deficient

Must be issued **within 30 days** and reported in **GSTR-1** of the month in which issued. Output tax is reversed in **GSTR-3B** for the same period.

Mandatory fields (per Rule 53):
- Name, address, GSTIN of supplier
- Nature of the document
- Consecutive serial number (≤ 16 chars)
- Date of issue
- Name, address, GSTIN of recipient
- Reference to original invoice (number + date)
- Taxable value, tax rate, tax amount
- Reason (excess tax / goods return / deficiency)
- Signature

---

### 4. Debit Note (Sec. 34(3) CGST)

Same as credit note but in the opposite direction — supplier raises against buyer when:
- Taxable value or tax on original invoice was less than required
- Quantity actually supplied was more than billed
- Price escalation clause kicks in

In this design, debit notes flow the *other* way too — Better Vision (as buyer) raises a debit note against a vendor for short shipment. In that case the GST output liability reduction is reflected in the issuer's GSTR-1.

ITC on the debited amount must be reversed in the issuer's GSTR-3B (Sec. 17(5)).

### 5. Goods Receipt Note (GRN) — internal document

GRN is not a statutory GST document but is **mandatory under most industry SOPs and audit standards**. It links:
- Purchase Order (commercial commitment)
- Vendor invoice (tax document)
- Physical goods received

Variance handling: If GRN qty < vendor invoice qty, a **debit note** is raised against the vendor. If GRN > vendor invoice (over-supply), it's typically not paid for and either returned or reconciled with a fresh invoice.

Retention: 7 years per **Rule 56 CGST** along with the linked purchase order and vendor invoice.

---

### 6. Purchase Order — commercial document

Not a statutory GST document. Falls under **Indian Contract Act 1872**. The vendor's acceptance (acknowledgement or first shipment) constitutes a binding contract.

Best practice fields:
- PO number + date
- Vendor full legal name, address, GSTIN, PAN
- Buyer (own) details with GSTIN, PAN, place of supply
- Ship-to address (if different)
- Line items with HSN, qty, rate, GST applicability, taxable value, total
- Payment terms (Net-30, Net-45 etc.)
- Delivery terms (DDP, FOB, etc.)
- Expected delivery date
- Currency (INR)
- Authorized signatory + seal placeholder

---

### 7. Day Book (Sec. 35 CGST r/w Rule 56)

Primary daily journal. Sec. 35 mandates every registered person to maintain:
- Production of goods
- Inward and outward supply of goods and services
- Stock of goods
- Input tax credit availed
- Output tax payable and paid

Rule 56 expands: every record shall be maintained for a period of 6 years from the due date of furnishing the annual return for the year (effectively 7 years from the event).

Day book entries cross-reference:
- POS Z-report (one Z per business date)
- GSTR-1 (auto-staged from invoices)
- Bank deposit slips

---

### 8. Z-Report (Day-end cash close — SOP-FIN-02)

Not statutory by itself but is the operational artifact of cash reconciliation. Cross-verifies:
- Tender split totals (cash / card / UPI / wallet / voucher) against POS ledger
- Counted cash against expected (opening + sales − refunds − expenses − deposits)
- HSN-wise output tax summary (which seeds GSTR-1 staging)

Variance > ±₹200 (Better Vision's policy threshold) requires ASM PIN sign-off.

### 9. Prescription Card (Optometry-specific)

Driven by **NCAHP Act 2021** and state council registration (e.g. Delhi Medical Council, DMC).

Mandatory practitioner fields on the Rx card:
- Practitioner name + qualifications
- State council registration number (e.g. `DMC/R-4412/2014`)
- **NCAHP UID** (e.g. `OPT-IN-22-04412`) — became mandatory for all practising optometrists in 2024
- Practice address
- Date of examination
- Signature + stamp

Mandatory patient + Rx fields:
- Name, age, sex
- Patient ID (internal)
- Chief complaint, last exam date
- Examination method (subjective, auto-refraction, slit-lamp, etc.)
- IOP, cover test, dilation status
- 8-column refraction grid: Eye / Sph / Cyl / Axis / Add / Prism / Base / VA / PD
- Lens specification (type / coating / tint / fitting height)
- Validity (typically 12 months from issue)

The patient is entitled to use this Rx with **any registered optician of their choice** — the card carries this statement.

**Drug Licence**: Stores dispensing contact lenses (Schedule H drug under D&C Rules) need a **Drug Licence**. Shown as `DL-OPT-GK1-2018-0421` on the Rx card.

### 10. Shelf Labels (Legal Metrology)

Under the **Legal Metrology (Packaged Commodities) Rules 2011**, every packaged retail commodity must display:
- Maximum Retail Price (MRP) **inclusive of all taxes**
- Net quantity / size
- Manufacturer name and address
- Date of manufacture / packaging
- Country of origin (for imports)

The shelf-label design carries the **MRP incl. tax** clearly, brand, model, color, size, and a Code-128-style barcode for the SKU.

---

### 11. Warranty Card

Driven by the **Consumer Protection Act 2019**. The warranty must clearly state:
- Coverage period (frame 12m, lens 6m in this design)
- What is covered
- What is excluded
- Claim procedure
- Customer + product identification linking to original invoice

Not a statutory tax document but a critical legal artifact for product liability.

---

### 12. Gift Voucher (Pre-paid Instrument)

In India, retail gift vouchers fall under **RBI's Pre-paid Payment Instruments (PPI) framework** when they exceed certain limits and are issued by non-banking entities. In-store closed-loop gift vouchers (redeemable only at the issuer's outlets) are **exempt** if value ≤ ₹10,000 per voucher.

GST: Per **CBIC Circular 196/08/2022**, vouchers themselves are not "goods" or "services" at issuance — GST is payable at redemption on the underlying supply. The voucher print template reflects this with the note "Tax payable on redemption per item HSN".

Security features (visual anti-counterfeit):
- Check digits (`7142-8810`)
- Serial barcode at bottom
- Guilloché-style background hatching (CSS `repeating-linear-gradient`)
- Bearer instrument warning

---

### 13. Customer + Vendor Statements

Not statutory but common business practice. Vendor statement specifically:
- Aging buckets (Current / 31–60 / 61–90 / 90+) — drives AP payment priority
- Outstanding balance with Dr/Cr indication
- Cross-references to all bills, GRNs, debit notes, payments in the period
- Reconciliation note ("Please report discrepancies within 15 days, else statement is treated as accepted")

Customer statement similar but B2C-flavored:
- Wallet balance
- Loyalty points (BV-Points) with earn / redeem / expire breakdown
- YTD spend + lifetime value
- NPS rating

## When to **not** copy this design as-is

Some fields and behaviours are stylized to look real but should not be lifted as legal language:

- **Sample numbers** (GSTIN, PAN, CIN, DMC reg, NCAHP UID) — replace with real entity values
- **Sample policy text** in declarations — review with the legal team
- **Tolerance thresholds** (cash variance ±₹200, margin gate 25%) — set per company policy
- **Retention reference** (7 years) — verify against current Rule 56 + state-specific requirements
