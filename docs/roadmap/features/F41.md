## Intended behavior (full intent)

IMS automatically identifies patients who have gone **24+ months with neither a confirmed order nor a prescription exam**. These are clinically lapsed patients — their Rx has expired and they have not returned. The system executes a two-wave tiered reactivation:

**Wave W1 (first contact):** Patient receives a WhatsApp/SMS with a 10% voucher valid 30 days, applicable on non-luxury items. A single-use voucher is minted and its code embedded in the message.

**Wave W3 (escalation, sent 14 days after W1 if no engagement):** If the patient did not convert (no order placed against the W1 voucher's campaign cycle) within 14 days, a second message goes out with a 20% voucher — increased urgency. W3 never fires if the patient already made a purchase after W1.

**Cooldown:** Once a patient enters a reactivation cycle (W1 sent), they are locked out of another full cycle for **180 days**. This prevents harassment. The 30-day TRAI/E6 frequency cap also applies on top.

**Luxury-brand exclusion:** Vouchers are NOT applicable toward items from the LUXURY_BRAND_CAPS list (Cartier, Chopard, Bvlgari, Gucci, Prada, Versace, Burberry) or the LUXURY discount_category. This exclusion is enforced at redemption in `redeem_voucher_atomic`, not at voucher issuance.

**Cost-floor guard (CORRECTIONS.md P1):** The voucher discount (W1=10%, W3=20%) must not push an item below cost+10% per category. This is checked at order-create time via the existing POS floor guard once the cost+10% board item is implemented; F41 is not responsible for implementing the full cost+10% engine (that is a separate board item), but must NOT claim the floor is already enforced — it is not. F41 must add an explicit `NOTE` comment in the voucher redemption path flagging this gap.

**Configuration:** All thresholds are owner-tunable via a settings doc (`lapsed_reactivation_settings`): lapse threshold months, W1/W3 discount %, W3 delay days, voucher validity, store scope, excluded brands list, channel, template IDs. The feature is **off by default** (`enabled=False`); the owner flips it on.

**Segment-level:** via E2 per-Store/Category/Brand scoping — the `applicable_stores` list restricts which stores participate; per-category and per-brand exclusions are applied at voucher redemption.

**Audit everything:** every voucher mint, every send, and every settings change is immutably logged.
