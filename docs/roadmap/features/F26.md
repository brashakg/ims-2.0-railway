## Intended behavior (full intent)

**Discount override:**
When a cashier at POS requests a discount that exceeds their role cap, the POS must not hard-fail. Instead:
1. The server returns a signal (a `request_id`) and creates a REQUESTED approval record in `approval_requests`.
2. POS renders a blocking overlay: item, proposed %, estimated margin, countdown (60 min). The cashier cannot proceed or submit the order until the request resolves.
3. The system fires an in-app bell notification and (DISPATCH_MODE=live) a WhatsApp to every eligible approver (ADMIN + SUPERADMIN) who covers that store.
4. The approver — from any browser, even on their phone — sees the request in their Approvals inbox, enters their PIN, and the request atomically transitions to APPROVED, minting a one-time `approval_token`.
5. The POS cashier enters the token (or the UI polls and resolves automatically); `cart_discount_approved_by` is stamped with the real approver's user_id; the order proceeds.
6. If the request is rejected or expires (60 min TTL, TASKMASTER sweep), the cart discount is reset to the role cap and the cashier is informed.
7. Every transition (REQUESTED, APPROVED, REJECTED, EXPIRED, CONSUMED) writes an immutable hash-chained `audit_logs` row. The approver's PIN hash is never stored in any audit row.

**The 20% margin trigger (DECISIONS-locked):** the above flow activates when the effective discount pushes the line margin below 20% (i.e. unit sell price < cost × 1.25). **Leave approval — remote fast-path:**
